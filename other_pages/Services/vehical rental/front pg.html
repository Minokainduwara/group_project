To Passengers

Buckle Up:

Remind passengers to always wear their seat belts while the vehicle is in motion for their safety. Encourage the use of seat belts in all seating positions, including back seats.

No Smoking Policy:

Inform passengers about the strict no-smoking policy inside the rental vehicle to maintain cleanliness and prevent damage. Advise passengers to refrain from smoking or vaping during the journey.

Child Safety:

Provide guidance on the use of child safety seats or boosters for young passengers according to local regulations. Ensure that children are properly secured in approved safety seats based on their age, weight, and height.

Respect the Vehicle:

Request passengers to treat the rental vehicle with care and respect to avoid damage or additional charges. Encourage passengers to avoid eating messy foods, spilling beverages, or causing any unnecessary wear and tear.

No Unauthorized Drivers:

Emphasize that only authorized drivers listed on the rental agreement are allowed to operate the vehicle. Advise passengers not to allow anyone else, including friends or family members, to drive the rental vehicle without prior approval.

Follow Traffic Laws:

Remind passengers to obey traffic laws, speed limits, and road signs for a safe and legal journey. Encourage passengers to be attentive and cooperative with the driver to ensure a smooth ride.

Emergency Procedures:

Provide instructions on what to do in case of emergencies such as accidents, breakdowns, or medical incidents. Share emergency contact numbers, roadside assistance details, and evacuation procedures if needed.

Personal Belongings:

Advise passengers to keep track of their personal belongings and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight in the vehicle. Remind passengers to check for any personal items before exiting the vehicle at the end of the journey.

Feedback and Assistance:

Encourage passengers to provide feedback on their rental experience and any issues encountered during the journey. Offer assistance and support for any concerns or questions passengers may have during the rental period.

To Drivers

Vehicle Inspection:

Instruct drivers to conduct a thorough inspection of the rental vehicle before driving, checking for any existing damage, tire pressure, fluid levels, lights, and other essential components. Encourage drivers to report any issues or concerns about the vehicle's condition before starting their journey.

Driving Responsibly:

Emphasize the importance of driving responsibly, following speed limits, traffic signs, and rules of the road at all times. Remind drivers to maintain a safe following distance, avoid distractions, and stay focused on the task of driving.

Seat Belt Usage:

Require drivers and all passengers in the vehicle to wear seat belts while the vehicle is in motion, regardless of the seating position. Stress the significance of seat belt usage for personal safety and compliance with the law.

No Alcohol or Drugs:

Prohibit drivers from operating the vehicle under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or any substances that may impair their driving abilities. Encourage drivers to make responsible decisions and arrange for alternative transportation if they are unable to drive safely.

Authorized Drivers Only:

Specify that only drivers listed on the rental agreement are permitted to operate the rented vehicle. Advise drivers not to allow unauthorized individuals to drive the vehicle to avoid liability issues.

Parking and Security:

Provide guidance on safe parking practices, including locking the vehicle, rolling up windows, and securing valuables to prevent theft or break-ins. Advise drivers to park in well-lit areas and follow any specific parking regulations or restrictions in the rental agreement.

Maintenance and Emergencies:

Instruct drivers to monitor the vehicle's performance during the rental period and promptly report any mechanical issues or warning lights. Provide instructions on how to handle emergencies such as accidents, breakdowns, or flat tires, including contacting roadside assistance or emergency services.

Return Procedures:

Communicate the return procedures for the rental vehicle, including fuel level requirements, cleanliness expectations, and any specific instructions for returning the keys. Remind drivers to return the vehicle on time to avoid additional charges and ensure a smooth transition for the next customer.

Customer Support:

Offer drivers access to customer support services for assistance with directions, vehicle operation, maintenance issues, or any other concerns that may arise during the rental period. Provide contact information for customer support channels to help drivers resolve issues promptly
