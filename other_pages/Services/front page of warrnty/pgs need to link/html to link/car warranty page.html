Car Warranty

1. Basic or Limited Warranty:

This warranty, also known as a bumper-to-bumper warranty, covers most vehicle components for a specific period, usually around 3 years or 36,000 miles.

2. Powertrain Warranty:

This warranty focuses on the major components that power the vehicle, such as the engine, transmission, and drivetrain. It often lasts longer than the basic warranty, typically around 5 years or 60,000 miles.

3. Corrosion Warranty:

Protects against rust and corrosion damage to the vehicle's body panels. The duration of this warranty varies by manufacturer.

4. Roadside Assistance:

Many warranties include roadside assistance services, such as towing, fuel delivery, jump-starts, and flat tire changes in the event of a breakdown.

5. Extended Warranty:

An optional coverage that extends beyond the standard warranty period, providing additional protection for an extra cost.

6. Emissions Warranty:

Ensures compliance with government emissions regulations and covers components related to emissions control.

7. Rust Perforation Warranty:

Specifically covers rust that penetrates through the body of the vehicle from the inside out.

8. Manufacturer Defect Coverage:

Covers repairs resulting from faulty materials or workmanship from the manufacturer.

9. Catalytic Converter Warranty:

Covers the catalytic converter, a critical emissions control component in the vehicle's exhaust system.
